Frequently Asked Questions

When should I start solid foods?

Most babies are ready for solid foods around 6 months of age, when they can sit up with support and show interest in food.

How often should I feed my newborn?

Newborns typically need to feed every 2-3 hours, or 8-12 times per day. Follow your baby's hunger cues.

Parenting Tips

Sleep Schedule

Establish a consistent bedtime routine to help your baby develop healthy sleep patterns.

Bonding Time

Skin-to-skin contact and talking to your baby helps with emotional development and bonding.

Myth Breaker

Myth: Babies need water in hot weather

Fact: Breast milk or formula provides all the hydration babies need in their first 6 months.

Basic Food Suggestions

6-8 months

Single ingredient purees: sweet potato, banana, avocado

8-10 months

Combination foods and soft finger foods

Hygiene Tips

Hand Washing

Always wash hands before handling your baby or preparing food.

Diaper Care

Change diapers frequently and clean thoroughly to prevent rashes.

WHO Guidelines

Exclusive Breastfeeding

WHO recommends exclusive breastfeeding for the first 6 months of life.

Vaccination Schedule

Follow the immunization schedule recommended by WHO and your healthcare provider.
